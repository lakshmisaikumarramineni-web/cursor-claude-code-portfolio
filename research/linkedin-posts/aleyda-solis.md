51 Minutes Ago

Are affiliate sites really "dead" because of AI Overviews? Not all of
them! For example: RunRepeat, Pack Hacker or CleverHiker are still
growing YoY, even though AI Overviews now appear on 81--95% of their top
non-branded queries based on
Similarweb traffic
data.

A few insights, patterns and learnings:

1. AI Overviews have a high presence in their SERPs and they're still
growing but... they're far more substitutive when the user only needs
a quick answer, and much less so when a confident purchase decision is
needed.

Looking at each site's top 100 non-branded keywords, an AI Overview is
present on: RunRepeat: 95%, Pack Hacker: 81%, CleverHiker: 81%.

Keywords with an AIO are half to two-thirds of their estimated organic
clicks... and yet, organic search traffic YoY:

CleverHiker: +70%
Pack Hacker: +28%
RunRepeat: +6% (at a much larger scale, nearly 1M additional organic
visits)

2. They're still capturing product discovery demand from Google with a
high share of non-branded search traffic: Pack Hacker: 91.9%,
CleverHiker: 98.1%, RunRepeat: 91.2%. They're not growing just because
people already know them.

3. Their visibility is concentrated in purchase decision queries per
Similarweb's intent classification: Pack Hacker: 72.7%, RunRepeat:
71.0%, CleverHiker: 64.9%.

These are queries where users want to compare, validate, check real
experience, understand trade-offs ... and then buy (rather than a quick
answer).

4. Their top pages are decision support ones, helping users make a
confident product decisions:

* CleverHiker: ~87% of organic landing page clicks go to tested "best
X" guides and category pages.
* Pack Hacker: Roughly a 50/50 split between buying guides and
reviews.
* RunRepeat: ~70% goes to individual model review pages: lab-tested,
measured, scored.

These pages provide in-depth, first-hand insights, a variety of images
and many times videos as well.

5. The three sites show more keyword losses than wins, and fewer
keywords holding positions 1--3 than a year ago: Their traffic is
growing while their ranking keywords decrease.

The shallow long tail is eroding (that's where AI answers impact), and
traffic is concentrating into fewer, stronger decision support pages.

6. The strongest sites are also building brand recognition, repeat
visits and audience loyalty: the 3 sites are also building demand beyond
Google, with an important direct traffic YoY growth:

CleverHiker: +76%
RunRepeat: +24%
Pack Hacker: +23%

For example, Pack Hacker also has a community/membership program with
access to exclusive content and better deals.

7. The above shows that affiliate sites that have become trusted product
research destinations with first-hand testing, real reviews, useful
comparisons, strong UX and a recognizable brand; are still growing, even
with AIOs on top of their money queries.

The TL;DR: The future of affiliate SEO is becoming the site users still
want to click after seeing the AI answer because a summary isn't
enough.
