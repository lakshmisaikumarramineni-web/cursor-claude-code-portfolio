Despite really enjoying this time and technology I do feel that
generative AI in search is a threat to critical thinking.

George Vlasyev

Are we sacrificing critical thinking for convenience? 🛑

For decades, we grew up with Google giving us a list of websites to
visit. We clicked, we compared, and through the process of searching, we
actually learned a lot about a subject by weighing different opinions.

Now? AI just hands us a direct answer.

On the latest episode of The GEO Blueprint, Michael
King shares a
sobering take: "It makes us stupider, actually... Now you have bots
interpreting that information for you, and they effectively draw the
conclusion... I think it is a recipe for more ignorance."

We are trading exploration for quick blurbs. Is this evolution, or are
we just getting lazier?
