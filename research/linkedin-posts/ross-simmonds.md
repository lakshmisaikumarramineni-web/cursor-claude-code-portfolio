The metrics that your CMO cares about are broken. The metrics that your
director of marketing care about, broken. They are all broken. The
metrics that we have cared about for so long are broken. They're
incomplete. Page views, keyword ranking, domain authority, organic
traffic, bounce rate versus the amount of people who are visiting our
site. These are the old metrics, but these metrics are broken. We used
to pop the bubbly over these metrics. We used to get excited about these
metrics and say, Page views is ROI, traffic as ROI, when in reality, ROI
is all about pipeline. It's all about pipeline, it's all about
revenue, it's all about actually driving sales. The return on
generative engine optimization is an opportunity, an opportunity for all
of us to walk away from the vanity metrics and to instead, embraced a
new scorecard, a scorecard that is rooted in a few things that
ultimately will help us better understand if we are getting closer to
true return on investment. We're going to be looking at AI citation
rates. So ROGO, if we want to call it that, Return on Generative Engine
Optimization, and I don't care if you want to call it AI LLM
optimization or AEO, whatever you want to call it.

It starts with AI Citation Rate. This is a metric that I want you to
incorporate in your scorecard. How often is your brand, your website,
being cited? Track over 200 different variations and prompts and get a
sense of how often is your website actually being cited in the LLMs.
Then you want to look at citation share versus your competitors. What is
the percentage of category-related citations that you get versus
competition? Upload 200 different prompts, all commercial intent,
transactional intent, bottom of funnel intent, so we can ensure that
they are rooted in what will drive pipeline. Then you're going to look
at mention rate velocity. How often are you being mentioned because of
content that's showing up on Reddit, on LinkedIn, on G2, on Medium, on
other sites that are starting to show up because you're running this
report in this scorecard regularly. Then we need to track AI referral
conversion. What percentage of your traffic, the metric that we used to
care about, the traffic metric, the organic metric, what percentage of
referral traffic from AI is actually converting to an MQL or
sales-accepted lead. Then finally, what is the pipeline total from AI
source?

You're not just looking at whether or not ChatGPT, LLMs, Grok, Claude,
Perflexity, are driving and referral traffic that converts. You're also
looking at giving your users the ability to self-attribute to whether or
not they actually spend time in an LLM beforehand. Give them the ability
to say, Where did you hear about us? And select the LLM of choice. How
much pipeline is actually attributed to AI referral traffic? Use CRM
tools like HubSpot to actually track first touch, multi-touch, and last
touch, and how LLMs might play a role in that. The formula that we start
to embrace for a row geo, ROI of generative engine optimization,
pipeline from AI sources, divided by the investment that you make in
GEO, SEO, distribution, etc. That is the math. When you take the total
volume of your pipeline from these sources, divided by your investment
and geo and distribution, et cetera, you start to see the ROGE, ROGE of
your work. That's it. The timeline for this, folks, is consistent. You
create great content once, you distribute forever. It is a mantra that I
started to preach back in 2018, and it is more relevant now than ever
before.

Why? Because content freshness matters. 50% of the most cited content in

and driving more citations in the LLMs. And guess what people are asking
for when they're talking to their LLMs? They're asking for the most
recent updates. What is the best company to serve me in X, Y, and Z
today? Folks, freshness matters. In an age in which AI is influencing
your buyers, you need to throw away the old path. It's no longer going

you can believe and assume that you are going to influence them by
simply writing a few blog posts, building a couple of links, and driving
ROI. It's not that simple, so embrace Rogeo.
