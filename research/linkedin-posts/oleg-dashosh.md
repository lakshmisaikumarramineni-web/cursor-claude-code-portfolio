Do not buy aged Reddit accounts. It's the biggest legal scam in this
industry.

Quick story first.

I met a guy at an SEO conference in Arizona a few months back. We talked
Reddit for a good hour and I basically sold him on the idea of promoting
his stuff there. He was excited. Went home, made a fresh account,
started posting links right away. Banned within days. Classic.

So he messages me asking about the best marketplaces to buy aged Reddit
accounts. I told him straight up, don't do it. He did it anyway. Bought
10 aged accounts for $3,500 total.

Every single one got banned within weeks. All 10. That's $3,500 gone
with zero recourse, because guess what, you can't exactly file a
complaint about your black market Reddit account purchase.

So why aged accounts almost always die?

The moment you buy an aged account you think you hit the jackpot. High
karma, years of history, trusted profile. You can finally share links
and stuff, right?

Wrong. Reddit's anti-spam systems are built specifically to catch this.
Here's what flags you:
1. Email change. First thing buyers do is swap the email to their own.
Reddit sees a sudden email change on a dormant account as a giant red
flag.

2. IP and location jump. The account posted from Ohio for 4 years and
now it's logging in from Vancouver, or worse, from a VPN or datacenter
IP. Instant flag. Reddit tracks login patterns and most sellers use
cheap proxies that are already blacklisted.

3. Device fingerprint change. New browser, new device, new everything.
Reddit fingerprints devices and links accounts that way. It's also how
they catch ban evasion, so the tech is mature.

4. Behavior shift. Account was quiet for 8 months, then suddenly posts 5
links a day in subreddits it never touched before. No human behaves like
that and Reddit knows it.

5. The account was garbage to begin with. Most marketplace accounts are
farmed by bots reposting old viral content for karma. Reddit's systems
often already have them flagged, they just haven't pulled the trigger
yet. You're buying a dead account walking.

6. Same account sold twice. Shady sellers resell the same account to
multiple buyers, or keep the recovery email and take it back after you
pay. Happens constantly.

7. Mods catch you too. It's not just admins. Mods of decent subreddits
check post history. An account that farmed karma on r/aww and now posts
SaaS links sticks out immediately, and one mod report can trigger an
admin review of everything.

In addition, when Reddit bans a purchased account, they often ban
everything associated with it. Your IP, your device, sometimes your
legit accounts too. My conference guy nuked his ability to start clean
because all 10 accounts were tied to his home IP.

What to do instead? Comment REDDIT MARKETING and I'll share a boring
but effective rules on what to do.

Anyone here actually had a purchased account survive long term?
Genuinely curious, because I've never seen it.

Silently.
