# B2B SaaS Marketing Research — AI-Powered SEO Content Production

## What this is
A research collection of 10 practitioners actively working in AI-powered SEO / GEO
(Generative Engine Optimization) content production, gathered as raw material for
a future marketing playbook.

## Why these 10 experts
Chosen for hands-on practice, not just commentary — people publishing original data,
running real client work, or building tools in this space right now:

- **Amanda Natividad** — Chief Evangelist, SparkToro; coined "Zero Click Marketing"
- **Kevin Indig** — Growth Memo newsletter; publishes original AI-citation research (Semrush/AirOps studies)
- **Oleg (dashosh)** — Runs r/AISEOforBeginners on Reddit; tracks real-time LLM citation patterns; also active on LinkedIn
- **Mike King** — Founder, iPullRank; built the "Relevance Engineering" framework
- **Lily Ray** — SEO/AI search lead, Amsive; widely cited on Google/AI search trust & spam issues
- **Aleyda Solis** — Founder, Orainti; hosts "Crawling Mondays" (weekly SEO/AI search video series)
- **Ryan Law** — Content lead, Ahrefs
- **Chris Long** — Nectiv; agency-side SEO experimentation
- **Garrett Sussman** — Practitioner-focused SEO/content commentary
- **Ross Simmonds** — Founder, Foundation Inc.; content distribution specialist

## What was collected
- `research/sources.md` — full expert list with profile links and annotations
- `research/youtube-transcripts/` — 18 video transcripts across 6 experts with active
  YouTube/podcast channels (Amanda, Mike King, Lily Ray, Aleyda, Chris Long, Ross Simmonds)
- `research/linkedin-posts/` — 65 LinkedIn posts across all 10 experts (5–8 posts each,
  depending on available video coverage), including native LinkedIn video transcripts
  and 2 PDF carousel posts fully summarized slide-by-slide, plus supporting images
  organized per author in `linkedin-posts/images/`

## Collection notes
- Experts with active YouTube/podcast channels got fewer LinkedIn posts (5); experts
  without video content got more (7–8) to compensate for the missing video signal.
- Lily Ray's YouTube content is 2+ years old, so her LinkedIn posts were weighted more
  heavily to capture her current thinking on AI search.
- Mike King's and Chris Long's video channels (iPullRank, Nectiv) are agency channels
  rather than personal ones — included since both are the primary practitioner voice
  behind them.
- LinkedIn content was manually collected (not scraped) to avoid LinkedIn ToS and
  account-risk issues tied to unofficial scraping tools.
- Two PDF carousel posts (Kevin Indig) were summarized directly from their source
  files rather than stored as raw PDFs in the repo, since the original files were
  ~85MB each (Canva export bloat).
